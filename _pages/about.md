Hi! I am **Mohammad Shamshad** 🌾 born 🎂 into a humble farming family :family: in Jamalpura village, Malerkotla region of Punjab, India 🇮🇳, was raised in a close-knit rural environment deeply connected to agriculture. 🌱 Early exposure to vegetable seedling production 🌱 and crop cultivation 🚜 within the family fostered a strong practical understanding of farming and village life, shaping interests 💡, values 📚, and long-term aspirations 🌿 ➝ 🌳 🏆.
{: .text-justify}

> An educational journey marked by perseverance and adaptability 💪📚 began at a local madrasa 🕌, completing primary education up to Class V, followed by studies at Islamia Senior Secondary School 🏫 up to Class VIII in the Urdu medium 📝. A transition to the Punjabi medium for Classes IX and X at the same institution reflected adaptability and determination 🔄✨. With a strong interest in science 🔬🧪, higher secondary education (+1 and +2) was pursued in the Medical stream 🩺 at a Government Senior Secondary School, demonstrating resilience, adaptability, and a passion for learning 🌱🚀.
{: .text-justify}

> My academic path has always been deeply rooted in my agricultural background 🌾, where hands-on experience in seedling production 🌱 sparked a lasting interest in plant sciences 🔬 and sustainable farming practices 🌍; with a strong foundation built on both practical exposure and formal education 📚, chose to pursue a career in agricultural sciences 🚜, and throughout my journey, A commitment to advancing agriculture 🌿 while remaining true to personal roots and values instilled from an early age 💚.
{: .text-justify}

Currently working as a **Sugarcane Breeder** 🌱 at **[Punjab Agricultural University (PAU), Ludhiana](https://www.pau.edu/), India** 🇮🇳, with joint appointments at the **[Regional Research Station, Kapurthala](https://pauwp.pau.edu/region-specific-research/)** and the **[Department of Plant Breeding and Genetics, PAU](https://pauwp.pau.edu/plant-breeding-genetics-home/)** 🎓; Leading sugarcane breeding research programs focused on developing high-yielding clones 🌾 with enhanced sugar content 🍬, strong resistance to diseases 🦠 and pests 🐛, improved ratooning ability (regrowth after harvest) 🌿, early maturity ⏱️, and adaptability to diverse agro-climatic conditions 🌍, while also emphasizing the broadening of the genetic base through germplasm conservation 🧬 and hybridization, and integrating advanced tools such as molecular markers 🔬, genomic selection 🧪, and biotechnology ⚗️ to accelerate breeding efficiency and genetic gain, ultimately developing varieties suitable for multiple uses including sugar, bioethanol ⛽, and bioenergy ⚡, thereby supporting sustainable agriculture 🌱 and contributing to societal welfare 🤝.
{: .text-justify}
During my tenure as a **Plant Breeder** 🌱 at the **[Regional Research Station, Faridkot](https://pau.edu/outstations/index.php?_act=manageOutstationData&DO=viewHomePage&intLinkID=3)**, PAU, in affiliation with the **[Department of Plant Breeding and Genetics, PAU](https://pauwp.pau.edu/plant-breeding-genetics-home/)** 🎓, was actively involved in cotton improvement programs 🌿 focused on the agro-ecological conditions of Punjab 🇮🇳; my work centered on developing and evaluating high-yielding cotton genotypes 🌾 with superior fiber quality traits such as staple length, strength, and fineness 🧵, while also contributing to hybridization and selection programs aimed at combining desirable agronomic performance with resistance to key pests like bollworms 🐛 and whitefly, as well as tolerance to cotton leaf curl virus (CLCuV) 🦠; additionally, participated in germplasm screening, field trials 🚜, and multi-location testing across environments to identify stable and adaptable lines, with further emphasis on early maturity ⏱️ and plant architecture suited to the cotton–wheat cropping system 🌾🌾, along with the application of modern breeding tools 🔬 to enhance selection efficiency and accelerate varietal improvement.
{: .text-justify}
Worked as a Post-Doctoral Researcher 🎓 at the TIGR<sup>2</sup>ESS programme, [University of Cambridge](https://tigr2ess.globalfood.cam.ac.uk/staff/dr-mohammad-shamshad) 🇬🇧, focusing on enhancing wheat resilience 🌾 to heat 🔥 and drought 💧 stress—key challenges under climate change 🌍. Research involved identifying genetic 🧬, physiological 🧪, and molecular ⚗️ mechanisms underlying stress tolerance, including gene mapping for heat and drought resilience, application of genetic markers in breeding 🌱, and analysis of traits such as water-use efficiency 💧 and root architecture 🌿. Contributed to the development of climate-resilient wheat varieties 🌾, supporting sustainable agriculture 🌍 and global food security 🍞.
{: .text-justify}
My work focuses 🎯 on the development and evaluation of high-yielding, disease-resistant, and abiotic stress-tolerant crops. [shamshad.in](https://www.shamshad.in) is my blog to keep track 🗒️ of my projects and different HOW-TOs related to data 🔬 and tech 📡.
{: .text-justify}
I am passionate about Agriculture and cutting-edge technologies. Having a solid 🖊️ understanding and rigorous 📌 hands-on experience in plant breeding, quantitative genetics, computational biology ⚙️ and high-throughput phenotyping, earned during my academic and research work with plant breeders/geneticists, bioinformaticians, and pathologists across my undergraduate, graduate, and postdoctoral career.
{: .text-justify}
Professional expertise in Plant Breeding, Quantitative Genetics, Genomics, and Computational Biology. Strong interest in experimental design, data analysis, and visualization using R, SAS, JMP, Python, UNIX/Linux, HTML/CSS, Git, and GitHub. Hands-on laboratory experience in DNA extraction, PCR, gel electrophoresis, and microscopy 🔬.
{: .text-justify}
